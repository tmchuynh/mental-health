2020 MENTAL HEALTH BY THE NUMBERS

    Among US Asolescents (ages 12 - 17):
  • 1 in 6 experienced a major depressive episode
  • 3 million had serious thoughts of suicide
  • 31% increase in mental health-related emergency department vists
    Among US Young Adults (aged 18 - 25):
  • 1 in 3 experienced a mental illness
  • 1 in 10 experienced a serious mental illness
  • 3.8 million had serious thoughts of suicide

ANNUAL PREVALENCE AMONG US ADULTS
BY CONDITION

    RECOGNIZING THE IMPACT
  • People with depression have 40% higher risk of developing cardiovasular and metabolic diseases than the general population
  • 32.1% of US adults with mental illness also experience a substance use disorder in 2020 (17 million individuals)
  • The rate of unemployment is higher among US adults who have mental illness (6.4%) compares to those who do not (5.1%)
  • Students aged 6-17 with mental, emotional, or behavioral concerns are 3x more likely to repeat a grade
  • 20.8% of people experiencing homelessness in the US have a serious mental health condition
  • 70% of youth in the juvenile jutice system have a diagnosable mental health condition
  • Depression and anxiety disorders cost the global exconomy $1 trillion in lost productivity each year
  • Depression is a leading cause of disability worldwide
  • 90% of people who die by suicide had shown symptoms of a mental health condition, according to interviews with family, friends and medical professionals
  • 46% of people who die by suicide had a diagnosed mental health condition
